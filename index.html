Core Leadership Training

One full day per week is dedicated to core leadership training facilitated by Academy Faculty. The curriculum is taught via lectures, seminars and group sessions.

Bibical Overview

‘What you say goes, God, and stays, as permanent as the heavens. Your truth never goes out of fashion; it’s as up-to- date as the earth when the sun comes up. Your Word and truth are dependable as ever’

— Psalm 119:89-90 MSG

God’s word is eternal. The Bible provides your continuing faith and growth. Every part of Scripture is God-breathed and useful in showing us the truth, correcting our mistakes and training us to live God’s way. Through His Word we are put together and shaped up for the tasks God has for us (2 Timothy 3:16- 17). The Bible is our authority in all matters of faith and life and feeding on the Bible is the way to be ‘thoroughly equipped us for every good work’.

During the first term of the core leadership training students will spend time reviewing and understanding to a deeper level key areas of the Bible. Students will be reminded that the Bible’s aim is to point us to Christ.

The keys areas covered during this term are:

• Basic theology, including the nature of God, ecclesiology and eschatology
• An Old Testament survey
• Nehemiah
• The Gospels
• Acts
• Corinthians
• The Epistles

‘Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light for my path’ Psalm 119:105

— Psalm 119:105

Personal Growth

“Success is... knowing your purpose in life, growing to reach your maximum potential, and sowing seeds that benefit others.”

— John Maxwell

Good leadership is vital at all times, in all places and in areas of life.

During this term of the core leadership training students spend time understanding what good leadership is and what it entails. Students will learn what the personal qualities and skills are that that increase our influence. Students will understand that before we can effectively lead others, we need to first learn how to lead in our own life.

Key areas covered during this term include:

• Preparation of a Leader
• Character
• Team
• Developing Emerging Leaders
• Vision

In addition with the benefit of a structured test and assessment, students will understand where they are presently and how they can further their personal development and growth. This will also involve identifying and assessing the unique gifts God has graced us with individually.

Church Stategy

‘Now you are the body of Christ, and each one of you is a part of it’

— 1 Corinthians 12:27

Jesus taught us that the church is one body with many parts. This means we all have different gifts and different tasks to perform – every gift and task is important. We are called to use our spiritual gifts well and to serve one another.

The operational responsibilities of a church are huge. To enable the church to accomplish its mission, people need to view their work in the church as an act of service. Your area of ministry is your way to serve the church and those outside the church.

In this term of the core leadership training students will be equipped with the skills necessary to effectively deal with a variety of ministry settings, and to identify where they may be called to serve.

Key areas covered during this term include:

• Understanding Church culture
• Communication
• Strategic Planning and Project Management
• Anointed in the Marketplace
• An overview of the following ministries:
  • Worship
  • Children
  • Youth
  • Young Adults
  • Stewardship

Missional Outreach

‘Then Jesus came to them and said “All authority in heaven and on earth has been give to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you”’

— Matthew 28:18-20

‘God blessed them and said to them, “Be fruitful and increase in number”’

— Genesis 1:28

From the beginning of time, mankind has a mandate to fill the earth and to bring God’s rule and reign to it. Jesus reiterates this when He declares “All authority in heaven and on earth has been given to me. Therefore go and make disciples of all nations…”.

During this term of the core leadership training students will learn how to reach out to and influence the local community as well as to reach out to a wider extent.

Key areas covered during this term include:

• What is mission?
• Local community projects and stories
• How do we support overseas mission and the persecuted church
• Church planting and multi-site churches
• What is social justice and our response to Micah 6:8 ‘to act justly and to love mercy and to walk humbly with your God’
